History and information about Oranges

314 B.C. - Now

The orange is the fruit of various citrus species in the family Rutaceae;
The orange is a hybrid between pomelo (Citrus maxima) and mandarin (Citrus reticulata).

The orange originated in a region encompassing Southern China,Northeast India, and Myanmar. As of 1987, orange trees were found to be the most cultivated fruittree in the world. Orange trees are widely grown in tropical and subtropical climates fortheir sweet fruit. The fruit of the orange tree can be eaten fresh, or processed for itsjuice or fragrant peel. As of 2012, sweet oranges accounted for approximately 70% of citrusproduction.

Orange History

  • 314 B.C. - The earliest mention of the sweet orange in Chinese literature.
  • 9th century - During the period of the Emirate of Sicily, citrus fruits — among them the bitter orange — were introduced to Sicily.
  • 10th century - The Moors introduced the orange to the Iberian Peninsula which was known as Al-Andalus and started large scale cultivation, In Europe.
  • Late 15th century, beginning of the 16th century - Sweet orange was unknown until, Italian and Portuguese merchants brought orange trees into the Mediterranean area.
  • 1646 - The sweet orange become well known throughout Europe.
  • 1664 - When Louis XIV condemned his finance minister, Nicolas Fouquet for peculation (maladministration of the state's funds) and lèse-majesté (actions harmful to the well-being of the monarch), part of the treasures which he confiscated were over 1,000 orange trees from Fouquet's estate at Vaux-le-Vicomte.
  • 1707-1710 - Spanish missionaries brought orange trees to Arizona.
  • 1841 - A commercial orchard was established near present-day Los Angeles.
  • 1872 - Florida farmers obtained seeds from New Orleans, after which orange groves were established by grafting the sweet orange on to sour orange rootstocks.
